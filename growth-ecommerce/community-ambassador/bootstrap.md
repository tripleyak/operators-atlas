# Community / Ambassador Program — Bootstrap

> **Inherits from:** `~/.claude/templates/session-kickoff-bootstrap.md`
>
> **Use when:** launching or overhauling a community program (Discord, Circle, Slack, native) or ambassador / advocate network.

---

## Who this is for

Community managers, advocacy leaders, brand managers at B2C / B2B / creator-led brands. Programs span 12-24 weeks to launch + ongoing. Communities mature over years, not months.

## Opinionated defaults

### Community vs. adjacent functions

- **Community ≠ marketing.** Marketing broadcasts; community enables connection between members. Running community as a broadcast channel kills it.
- **Community ≠ customer support.** Support is 1-to-1 resolution; community is many-to-many learning + connection. Mixing destroys both.
- **Community ≠ product feedback.** Community *produces* feedback as a byproduct; if you run it primarily to extract feedback, members feel used.

### Platform choice

- **Discord:** best for developer, creator, consumer-tech, gaming communities; informal + real-time
- **Circle:** best for course/cohort/professional communities; structured + paid-gate compatible
- **Slack:** best for B2B / operator communities; familiar + threaded
- **Facebook Groups:** best for mass-consumer / older demographic; large reach but weak discovery
- **Native in-product:** best for SaaS with strong engagement; hard to bootstrap
- **Reddit:** best as supplemental — low ownership, high risk

### Health metrics

- **% of members posting in 30 days:** 5-15% is healthy; 15-25% is thriving; <5% is a broadcast channel
- **Response rate to questions:** within 24h for 80%+ of questions
- **New-member onboarding:** >50% of new members post within first 14 days, or the cohort is inert
- **Moderator ratio:** 1 active moderator per 500-1000 active members

### Ambassador program tiers

- **Seed (entry):** opted-in from existing customer base; perks + recognition
- **Active (contributing):** posts content, answers questions, refers customers; small rewards
- **Lead (content creator):** produces UGC, hosts events, represents at conferences; meaningful compensation
- **Evangelist (strategic):** co-creates with brand, appears at brand events, often formalized with contract

### Ambassador compensation

- **Perks + recognition** for seed/active tiers (gated content, early access, branded merch)
- **Small stipends + commission** for lead tier ($500-$2000/month typical + referral revenue share)
- **Formal contracts** for evangelist tier (retainer + performance bonus)
- **Never pay for authenticity** — creators who seem too sponsored lose community credibility

### Program don'ts

- **Don't fake community** — real communities have disagreement; moderated-to-agreement communities die
- **Don't promote launches through community first** — it feels like bait-and-switch
- **Don't gate community contribution on purchase** — gates kill discovery
- **Don't centralize too much** — empowered members scale; micromanaged ones leave

### Program measurement

- **Primary:** member engagement depth (posts per active, DMs initiated, events attended)
- **Secondary:** ambassador-driven content (UGC, reviews, referrals)
- **Tertiary:** pipeline / revenue impact (measurable but indirect)
- **Anti-metric:** follower / member count alone (vanity)

## Step 0 — Interview question additions

1. **Why a community?** — specific job-to-be-done: learning, connection, advocacy, feedback?
2. **Existing community-adjacent activity** — customer Slack, Facebook group, Discord server, Reddit presence?
3. **Community manager role** — dedicated hire, part of marketing team, agency-led?
4. **Platform preference + constraints** — integration, budget, user tech-savviness?
5. **Member source** — existing customers, waitlist, open signup?
6. **Ambassador program overlap** — merging with community or separate?
7. **Moderation capacity** — 24/7 coverage? Language coverage?
8. **Content calendar** — seeded content, AMA cadence, event cadence?
9. **Brand voice in community** — how informal, how opinionated?
10. **Crisis / brand-safety plan** — if something goes viral negatively?

## Step 2 — Milestone template (16-week launch)

```markdown
### M1 — Strategy + Platform Choice (week 1-3)
- Deliverable: community purpose statement; platform decision; moderation structure
- Validation: purpose passes "what job does this do for members?" test
- Target: end of week 3

### M2 — Platform Setup + Content Seed (week 3-6)
- Deliverable: platform configured; channels / threads designed; 30+ seed posts; onboarding flow
- Validation: structure feels inviting, not sterile; mod team trained
- Target: end of week 6

### M3 — Soft Launch (100-500 members) (week 6-10)
- Deliverable: invited cohort from existing customers; active engagement started
- Validation: 50%+ of invited members post in first 14 days
- Target: end of week 10

### M4 — Ambassador Program Design (week 8-12)
- Deliverable: tier structure, compensation model, application flow
- Validation: first 20-50 ambassadors identified + onboarded
- Target: end of week 12

### M5 — Open / Expanded Launch (week 12-15)
- Deliverable: open signup + promotion; growth to 1K-5K members
- Validation: health metrics trending right; mod capacity sufficient
- Target: end of week 15

### M6 — Event + Content Cadence (week 15-16+)
- Deliverable: AMAs, virtual events, UGC challenges, ambassador content
- Validation: sustainable cadence; engagement stable; ambassador contributions visible
- Target: end of week 16+
```

## Step 3 — Critical mental model

> **Community compounds only if members find value in each other — not just in the brand.**
>
> Brands that run community as an "extension of marketing" create ghost towns: the brand broadcasts, few members engage with each other, new members see a dead space and leave. The compound never starts.
>
> Brands that run community as "enabling member-to-member connection" produce the opposite: members answer each other's questions, befriend each other, recognize each other's expertise. The brand becomes a supporting presence, not the center.
>
> The tell: in a healthy community, most valuable conversations happen between members without brand involvement. The brand's job is to create the context — platform, norms, events, recognition — not to be in every conversation.

## Step 4 — Domain validators

- **% of active members posting** monthly — target 5-15% minimum
- **Response rate to questions** — 80%+ within 24h
- **New-member activation** — 50%+ post in first 14 days
- **Moderator capacity** — 1:500-1000 active members
- **Ambassador tier distribution** — healthy pyramid, not top-heavy

## Step 8 — Working protocol additions

- **Never broadcast-only in community** — the metric dies fast
- **Never centralize moderation** on the brand team past 1K members — empower member-moderators
- **Never promote a launch in community first** — bait-and-switch
- **Never delete constructive criticism** — moderated-to-agreement communities lose credibility
- **Always onboard new members intentionally** — welcome + intro post + first-contribution prompt
- **Always respond to brand tags** within 24h — shows brand presence without dominance
- **Always celebrate member contributions** publicly — recognition is the #1 retention lever
