# Launch / GTM Program — Bootstrap

> **Inherits from:** `~/.claude/templates/session-kickoff-bootstrap.md`
>
> **Use when:** starting a new product / brand / service launch across DTC, retail, Amazon, subscription, B2B SaaS, or marketplace contexts.

---

## Who this is for

Growth leaders, brand managers, product marketing, ecommerce leads, founders running a go-to-market program for a new product, new category extension, new market entry, or relaunch. Standard program is 90 days (30+30+30 pre-launch / launch / scale); complex launches extend to 120-180 days.

## Opinionated defaults

### Launch windows

- **90-day launch:** 30-day pre-launch + 30-day launch + 30-day scale
- **3 waves:** seed list → broader audience → paid acceleration
- **PR embargo: 72 hours pre-launch** for coordinated media
- **Hero assets created 30 days pre-launch** — video, photography, copy, landing page, A+ Content (Amazon)

### Decision gates

- **Day 0 gate:** hero content / inventory / channel configs ready. Miss this and launch slips, not pushes forward.
- **Day 30 gate:** Is initial demand validated? CAC on target? Retention signal positive? Decision: scale / iterate / kill.
- **Day 90 gate:** Systematic economics. LTV trajectory. Channel mix proven. Decision: scale aggressively / optimize / wind down.

### KPIs by stage

- **Pre-launch:** list growth, pre-order count (if applicable), waitlist size
- **Launch (Day 0-30):** Day-0 revenue, first-week sell-through, inventory burn rate, CAC, review velocity, PR mentions
- **Scale (Day 30-90):** CAC stability, blended MER, repeat-purchase rate emerging, channel expansion signals
- **Post-90:** LTV:CAC trajectory, organic rank, brand-search trend

### Launch risks to plan for

- **Inventory underestimation** — if product takes off, run-out kills momentum; over-order if signal is strong
- **PR timing collision** — don't launch the week of a competitor launch or major industry event
- **Platform policy snags** — pre-approve Amazon listings, Meta ads, Google Shopping compliance 14+ days before launch
- **Review burst management** — seed reviewers in advance; Vine enrollment on Amazon; trusted creators ship pre-launch
- **Channel readiness** — email list, SMS list, DTC site, Amazon listing, retail placement all must be live coordinated

### What not to do

- **Don't launch quietly hoping for organic** — without coordinated paid + PR + owned channels, launches underperform by 60-80%
- **Don't discount aggressively at launch** — anchors price expectations low; hard to unwind
- **Don't skip PR embargo structure** — uncoordinated media creates confusion
- **Don't launch in Q4 peak without 60-day lead time** — inventory, ad auction CPMs, freight delays all compound

## Step 0 — Interview question additions

1. **Product / brand category** — new SKU, new brand, category extension, geographic expansion?
2. **Channels in scope** — DTC, Amazon, retail, wholesale, marketplace, B2B?
3. **Existing audience / list** — how many existing customers / subscribers to lean on for seed wave?
4. **Target Day-30 revenue / AOV / units**
5. **Inventory commitment** — how many units? what's the risk if we over- or under-commit?
6. **Pricing + margin structure** — gross margin at launch, scale margin, promotion depth available
7. **Competitive launch timing** — any competitor launches or major events in the 90-day window?
8. **PR + influencer capacity** — existing relationships, agency engagement, budget
9. **Paid media readiness** — accounts structured, creative ready, compliance approved
10. **Retail / wholesale lead time** — if retail, when do POs need to land?
11. **Brand positioning clarity** — is the messaging locked, or still being tested?
12. **Risk appetite** — is this a bet-the-company launch or one of many SKU launches?

## Step 2 — Milestone template (90-day / 13-week program)

```markdown
### M1 — Pre-Launch Plan + Asset Production (week 1-4)
- Deliverable: launch plan (all channels), hero assets, landing page, email sequence, influencer packages
- Validation: hero video live, Day-0 channel content approved, ad campaigns configured but paused
- Target: end of week 4 (Day -7)

### M2 — Seed Wave + PR Embargo (week 4-5)
- Deliverable: seed list activated; PR embargo live; Day-0 countdown running
- Validation: pre-launch traffic at target; embargo press committed
- Target: end of week 5 (Day -1)

### M3 — Public Launch (week 5)
- Deliverable: Day-0 launch across all channels; PR mentions live; paid media launched
- Validation: Day-0 revenue hit target ±20%; no platform policy issues; inventory tracking
- Target: end of week 5 (Day 0)

### M4 — Scale Wave (week 6-9)
- Deliverable: paid media scaling by channel; creative iteration; retention flow activation
- Validation: Day-30 CAC <target; review velocity positive; no inventory crisis
- Target: end of week 9 (Day 30)

### M5 — Day-30 Gate Review + Iteration (week 9-10)
- Deliverable: Day-30 review; kill/iterate/scale decision; revised plan for next 60 days
- Validation: data-driven decision made; next-phase plan approved
- Target: end of week 10 (Day 37)

### M6 — Day-90 Retrospective + Scale Decision (week 13)
- Deliverable: Day-90 retrospective; LTV trajectory; channel mix; Year 1 plan
- Validation: full program learnings documented; next-phase funding / planning locked
- Target: end of week 13 (Day 90)
```

## Step 3 — Critical mental model

> **Launch day is not the end. It's the beginning of the real learning period.**
>
> The first 90 days reveal two things that pre-launch research cannot:
> 1. **Is there real demand?** Pre-orders and waitlists are interest; paid acquisition at target CAC is demand.
> 2. **Is the GTM motion efficient?** Channel mix, creative, pricing, audience — the combinatoric question only plays out with real spend.
>
> Treat launch as a test, not a coronation. The first 30 days are hypothesis validation; the next 60 days are systematic optimization. Expect the plan to change — locking in on day 0 assumptions is the failure mode.
>
> Most launches either: scale too fast on weak early signal (hubris) or too slow on strong early signal (caution). The Day-30 gate is designed to force an honest read.

## Step 4 — Domain validators

- **Hero asset readiness** — video, photography, copy, landing page, product listings (all channels) — 7 days pre-launch
- **Inventory position** — 60+ days of supply at target run-rate on launch day
- **PR embargo integrity** — embargo agreements signed, pre-launch previews seeded
- **Channel readiness** — ads approved, email sequence tested, SMS compliance clear, site load-tested
- **Review seeding** — Vine enrollment live (Amazon), seed reviewers shipped, micro-influencers under NDA
- **Day-0 logistics** — customer support staffed, fulfillment confirmed, shipping carriers notified

## Step 8 — Working protocol additions

- **Never launch without 60+ days of inventory** at target run-rate.
- **Never launch without a Day-30 gate pre-agreed** on what "success" means.
- **Never discount aggressively at launch** — anchors pricing low.
- **Never scale paid before Day-14** — insufficient signal.
- **Never ignore customer support tickets Day-0 through Day-7** — they are the leading indicator.
- **Always write Day-30 and Day-90 retrospectives** with data, not vibes.
- **Always communicate launch plan to CX / Support / Ops / Finance** — launches are cross-functional.
