## Use cases

- **Category SERP analysis** — what does the top-10 look like for target keywords?
- **Competitor content teardown** — cluster strategy, velocity, quality at category leaders
- **SEO tool evaluation** — Ahrefs vs. SEMrush vs. Moz; Clearscope vs. Surfer vs. Frase; content AI tools
- **Algorithm / update research** — core updates, helpful-content updates, product-reviews updates
- **Content AI landscape** — AI writing tools, AI editing tools, AI SEO assistants; what's working, what's a trap
- **E-E-A-T benchmarking** — what signals do high-authority sites have that yours doesn't?

## Opinionated source priorities

**Prioritize:**

- **Google Search Central** — official documentation, blog posts, Search Off the Record podcast
- **SEO research firms** — Ahrefs blog (data-heavy), Moz Whiteboard Friday, SEMrush studies
- **Operator voice** — Lily Ray, Marie Haynes, Kevin Indig, Eli Schwartz, Ross Hudgens — these operators publish primary research
- **Academic research on search** — Stanford, Cornell, Berkeley papers on information retrieval
- **Industry reports** — SparkToro, GrowAndConvert, Terakeet for enterprise SEO

**De-prioritize:**

- SEO "guru" YouTube content (heavy on tactics that get nuked)
- AI-generated SEO advice (ironic but rampant)
- Course-seller content (motivated sales bias)

## Question-set specializations

### For SERP analysis / competitor teardown:

1. Top-10 ranking pages for target keyword cluster
2. Word count / depth of top-ranking articles
3. Linked sources / citations per article
4. Author / E-E-A-T signals
5. Internal-linking architecture of top sites
6. Backlink profile of top-ranking pages (domains, DR, anchor diversity)
7. Content refresh cadence — how often is the top-5 updated?
8. Gap analysis — what intents are missing from the SERP?

### For algorithm / update research:

1. What specifically changed — ranking factors, content signals, technical signals?
2. Measured impact by vertical — which categories hit hardest?
3. Remediation playbook — what's Google signaling as the fix?
4. Timeline — is it fully rolled out, phased, or reversible?
5. Peer-brand recovery patterns — who's come back, what did they change?

### For content AI landscape:

1. Which tools actually pass Google's AI-detection / helpful-content bar?
2. Hybrid workflows (AI draft + human editor) vs. pure AI — measured ranking / traffic differences
3. Editorial-review effort required — time cost per article vs. fully-written
4. Pricing vs. output quality — ROI at scale
5. Risk — Google's public stance on AI content vs. actual enforcement patterns
