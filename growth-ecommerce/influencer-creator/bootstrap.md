# Influencer / Creator Program — Bootstrap

> **Inherits from:** `~/.claude/templates/session-kickoff-bootstrap.md`
>
> **Use when:** starting a multi-session influencer or creator-economy program — seeding, paid partnerships, whitelisting, affiliate / ambassador, creator-led UGC generation, or TikTok Shop creator program.

---

## Who this is for

Brand managers, influencer-marketing specialists, partnerships leads at DTC / consumer brands. Programs span 8-16 weeks for a new program launch, or ongoing for an established creator pipeline.

## Opinionated defaults

### Creator tiers + strategy

- **Nano (1K-10K followers):** seeding-first, high volume, low cost/per-post. Best for authentic UGC + product validation.
- **Micro (10K-100K):** paid partnerships possible, strong niche engagement. Workhorse tier for most DTC programs.
- **Mid-tier (100K-1M):** paid + whitelisting. Reach + brand-safe at measurable cost.
- **Macro (1M+):** celebrity-adjacent economics; requires performance clause or huge brand-lift case.

### Seed before you pay

- **Always start with seeding** (unpaid gifting) 30-60 days before paid program. Validates creator fit, identifies natural advocates, creates reference content.
- **10-30% of seeded creators typically opt in** to continued partnership. The other 70-90% aren't worth paying.

### Deal structure

- **Always include usage rights** — minimum 90 days paid amplification + 1 year organic usage.
- **Always include deliverables minimum** — 3 pieces of content per paid partnership (1 hero + 2 supporting).
- **Always include whitelisting / allowlisting rights** — paid amplification on creator's own handle outperforms branded paid by 2-3× typical.
- **Never pay flat fees without performance clause** unless celebrity tier with brand-lift study attached.
- **Never do one-off partnerships without continuation clause** — repeat creators outperform new creators.

### Measurement

- **Dedicated UTMs + dedicated discount codes** per creator. Without them, attribution is impossible.
- **Primary KPI: CAC on creator-driven traffic.** Secondary: engagement, reach, sentiment.
- **Report ratio: 80% performance, 20% brand-lift** for most DTC programs.
- **Creator ROI: ≥2× spend in first 90 days for established programs;** launch programs can run at 1× to build library.

### Brand safety + compliance

- **FTC #ad disclosure required** on every paid partnership — no creative license to hide it.
- **Content review before go-live** for paid content. Seed content can skip review but should be monitored.
- **Brand-safety screen:** past content audit, sentiment analysis, controversy check before any investment.
- **Creator-owned audience data required** — never trust platform-reported follower counts alone. Use Modash / Creatable / Grin / Aspire / HypeAuditor for fraud detection.

### Platform mix

- **Instagram + TikTok:** table stakes for most DTC in 2026
- **YouTube:** long-form for considered purchases; high-effort but long tail
- **TikTok Shop:** where creator + commerce merge; separate strategy
- **LinkedIn:** B2B only; different tier structure
- **Newsletter creators:** under-indexed; often best ROI in B2B + premium DTC

## Step 0 — Interview question additions

1. **Current creator program state** — seeding, paid, both, neither?
2. **Platform focus** — IG / TikTok / YouTube / Newsletter / TikTok Shop?
3. **Brand voice + creative guidelines** — how strict? Can creators be themselves?
4. **In-house capacity** — creator outreach, vetting, deal negotiation, content review?
5. **Tooling** — Grin / Aspire / Creatable / Modash / LTK / ShopMy / in-house spreadsheets?
6. **Measurement infrastructure** — are UTMs + discount codes attached to creators today?
7. **Past program performance** — any partnerships in the last 12 months; results?
8. **Budget envelope** — monthly creator-marketing budget; one-time launch push?
9. **Content-usage rights current state** — perpetual, time-limited, or uncleared?
10. **Affiliate / ambassador layer** — existing program, or merging with creator?
11. **Legal review capacity** — can contracts go through legal at the cadence needed?

## Step 2 — Milestone template (12-week program)

```markdown
### M1 — Program Design + Vetting Infrastructure (week 1-2)
- Deliverable: creator-tier strategy; vetting rubric; tooling selection; brief templates
- Validation: 3+ creator briefs written; vetting tool operational; tracking infrastructure ready
- Target: end of week 2

### M2 — Seeding Wave Launch (week 2-5)
- Deliverable: 50-200 creators seeded; content monitoring live
- Validation: 30%+ of seeded creators post organic content; 10-30% opt into continued partnership
- Target: end of week 5

### M3 — Paid Partnerships Wave 1 (week 5-8)
- Deliverable: 10-30 paid partnerships launched; UTMs + codes attached; content reviewed + approved
- Validation: First content live; performance tracking operational
- Target: end of week 8

### M4 — Whitelisting / Paid Amplification (week 6-10)
- Deliverable: top-performing creator content running as paid ads on creator handles
- Validation: whitelisted ads running; CAC comparable to branded paid or better
- Target: end of week 10

### M5 — Performance Review + Retention Tier (week 10-11)
- Deliverable: top creators identified for ambassador / long-term program
- Validation: data-driven kill/continue decisions; ambassador contracts drafted
- Target: end of week 11

### M6 — Ongoing Program + Reporting Cadence (week 12+)
- Deliverable: monthly reporting rhythm; quarterly creator refresh; ambassador program live
- Validation: program runs without external help; reporting auto-generates
- Target: end of week 12
```

## Step 3 — Critical mental model

> **Creator partnerships are content partnerships with amplification attached.**
>
> The content is the product; the audience is the distribution. Programs that treat creators as "influencers renting out their audience" underperform programs that treat creators as "content collaborators whose audience is a bonus."
>
> Pay-per-post flat-fee programs always under-deliver: creators optimize for the post, not the long-term relationship, and you end up with one-off content that doesn't compound.
>
> Whitelisting changes the economics. A mid-tier creator whose content runs on paid amplification from your brand's account (using creator handle + content) typically outperforms pure branded paid by 2-3× because the audience algorithm treats it as organic-adjacent. This is the underexploited leverage point in most programs.

## Step 4 — Domain validators

- **Vetting:** every creator passes fraud check (audience quality, engagement authenticity)
- **Tracking:** every paid creator has unique UTM + discount code; data flowing to dashboard
- **Content review:** every paid piece reviewed before go-live; FTC disclosure verified
- **Deliverables:** every contract meets minimum content count; usage rights signed
- **Brand safety:** monthly audit of creator past content + public statements

## Step 8 — Working protocol additions

- **Never skip vetting** — fraud is rampant, especially in 10K-100K tier
- **Never pay without UTM + discount code** — untrackable spend
- **Never let FTC disclosure slip** — regulatory risk is real and enforcement is real
- **Never pay flat without performance clause** (except celebrity tier)
- **Always test seeding first** — paid-first wastes money on bad fits
- **Always document creator-relationship notes** — who responds fast, who ghosts, who delivers on brief, who doesn't
- **Always capture usage rights** — content without rights can't be re-purposed
