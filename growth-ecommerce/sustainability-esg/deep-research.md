## Use cases

- **Certification landscape research** — which certifications fit your brand + category?
- **Scope 3 measurement methodology** — how to measure supply-chain emissions
- **Regulatory landscape** — FTC Green Guides, EU Green Claims Directive, UK CMA
- **Competitor ESG positioning teardown** — how are peers positioning + reporting?
- **Reporting framework selection** — TCFD vs. SASB vs. GRI vs. combinations
- **Greenwashing case study research** — what's triggered enforcement / lawsuits

## Opinionated source priorities

**Prioritize:**

- **Official regulatory sources** — FTC Green Guides, EU Commission, UK CMA, SEC climate-rule proposals
- **Standard-setters' publications** — SBTi, TCFD, SASB, GRI, CDP
- **B-Lab + B-Corp community** — certification data
- **Academic research on ESG** — MIT Sloan, Harvard, Yale Center for Business + Environment
- **Industry-specific sustainability reports** — Textile Exchange (apparel), Ellen MacArthur Foundation (circular), World Business Council for Sustainable Development
- **Journalistic investigation** — Bloomberg Green, Reuters Sustainability, The Guardian Environment

**De-prioritize:**

- Brand-published sustainability marketing
- Agency-published "how to be sustainable" content
- AI-generated ESG content

## Question-set specializations

### For certification landscape:

1. Certification options by category + ambition level
2. BIA / rubric requirements
3. Application fee + audit cost
4. Timeline to certification
5. Renewal / audit cadence
6. Brand-value lift (if measurable)
7. Retail-partner acceptance (does Whole Foods require this? does Target?)

### For scope 3 methodology:

1. GHG Protocol scope 3 categories
2. Supplier-specific vs. spend-based emission factors
3. Product carbon footprint (PCF) vs. organizational
4. Primary vs. secondary data tradeoffs
5. Measurement platforms (Watershed, Persefoni, Sustain.Life, Greenly)
6. Verification standards (ISAE 3410, ISO 14064)

### For regulatory landscape:

1. FTC Green Guides — current + pending revisions
2. EU Green Claims Directive — implementation timeline
3. UK CMA greenwashing enforcement actions
4. SEC climate disclosure rules
5. State-level (California, Colorado) sustainability requirements
6. International patchwork — Australia, Canada, Japan sustainability regulation

### For competitor teardown:

1. Public claims + substantiation visible
2. Certifications held
3. Reports published + framework used
4. Supply-chain transparency disclosures
5. Operational improvements documented
6. Stakeholder communication patterns

### For greenwashing case study:

1. Lawsuit / enforcement specifics
2. Claims that triggered action
3. Substantiation gaps identified
4. Settlement / penalty details
5. Industry ripple effects
