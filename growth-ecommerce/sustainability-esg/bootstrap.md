# Sustainability / ESG Program — Bootstrap

> **Inherits from:** `~/.claude/templates/session-kickoff-bootstrap.md`
>
> **Use when:** launching or overhauling sustainability positioning, ESG (environmental, social, governance) program, B-Corp certification, supply-chain transparency, or climate-reporting program.

---

## Who this is for

Sustainability leaders, brand marketers, CPG operators, B-Corp candidates. Programs span 12-36 weeks depending on depth — sustainability claims require operations to match.

## Opinionated defaults

### Claims-first philosophy

- **Don't claim what you can't prove.** Greenwashing is a legal + reputation risk; regulators (FTC Green Guides, EU Green Claims Directive, UK CMA) are enforcing.
- **Third-party certification > self-declaration.** B-Corp, Fair Trade, Organic, Regenerative Organic, Leaping Bunny, 1% for the Planet — certifications are credibility leverage.
- **Measurement before marketing.** Scope 1 / Scope 2 / Scope 3 emissions measured before claiming "carbon neutral" or "climate positive."
- **Operations must catch up before marketing** — don't out-sustain what your operations actually do.

### Certifications

- **B-Corp:** 80+ BIA (B Impact Assessment) score, legal form amendment (benefit corp), 3-year audit cycle; meaningful but not universal
- **Fair Trade / Rainforest Alliance / UTZ:** commodity-specific (coffee, cocoa, cotton)
- **Organic (USDA / EU):** category-gated claims
- **Regenerative Organic Certified (ROC):** soil health + animal welfare + social fairness; harder to obtain
- **1% for the Planet:** 1% of revenue to environmental causes; simple, transparent
- **Climate Neutral Certified / SBTi** (Science Based Targets initiative): climate-specific

### Scope 1/2/3 emissions

- **Scope 1:** direct emissions (facilities, vehicles you own)
- **Scope 2:** indirect emissions (purchased electricity)
- **Scope 3:** upstream + downstream (supply chain, shipping, customer use, end of life) — usually largest for consumer brands
- **Don't claim carbon neutral on scopes 1+2 only** — scope 3 is 70-95% of most consumer brands' footprint

### ESG reporting standards

- **TCFD (Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures):** governance, strategy, risk, metrics — increasingly required
- **SASB (Sustainability Accounting Standards Board):** industry-specific metrics
- **GRI (Global Reporting Initiative):** comprehensive sustainability reporting
- **CDP (formerly Carbon Disclosure Project):** climate + water + forests
- **B Corp BIA:** multi-dimensional assessment

### Supply chain transparency

- **Tier 1:** direct suppliers — most brands have visibility
- **Tier 2 + 3:** sub-suppliers, raw materials — increasingly expected
- **Full traceability** for high-risk ingredients/materials (cobalt, palm oil, cotton from Uyghur region, etc.)
- **Supplier code of conduct** required for credible claims

### Social dimension

- **Fair wages** through supply chain
- **Labor rights** — no forced labor, no child labor, safe working conditions
- **Diversity + inclusion** in workforce + suppliers + leadership
- **Community investment** — local hiring, community partnerships

### Governance dimension

- **Board composition + independence**
- **Executive compensation tied to ESG outcomes**
- **Whistleblower protections**
- **Anti-corruption policies**

### Consumer trust patterns

- **Gen Z values sustainability 2-3× more than Gen X/Boomer** — but will detect insincerity
- **Authenticity > marketing slickness** — imperfect but honest beats polished but vague
- **Progress narrative > perfection claim** — "here's where we are, here's where we're going" works
- **Specificity > generality** — "we cut emissions 40% since 2020" > "we care about the planet"

### Don'ts

- **Don't claim "sustainable" / "eco-friendly" / "natural" without specifics** — these terms are legally scrutinized
- **Don't use generic "green" imagery without substance** — greenwashing triggers regulatory attention
- **Don't launch ESG marketing before operations support it** — inverse relationship between sophistication + skepticism
- **Don't certify then stop improving** — certifications require continuous improvement

## Step 0 — Interview question additions

1. **Current sustainability state** — operations, certifications, public claims
2. **Driving force** — consumer demand, regulatory, supply-chain risk, investor pressure, founder values?
3. **Scope of commitment** — operations-first vs. marketing-first?
4. **Supply chain visibility** — tier 1, tier 2, tier 3 status
5. **Measurement capability** — emissions measured? Social metrics tracked?
6. **Certifications in scope** — B-Corp, Fair Trade, Organic, etc.
7. **Reporting ambition** — internal only, public report, financial disclosure, framework (TCFD, SASB, GRI)?
8. **Stakeholder pressure** — investors (IR team involved?), customers (surveys, complaints?), regulators?
9. **Competitive context** — peer-brand positioning on sustainability
10. **Legal / compliance capacity** — can navigate greenwashing risk?

## Step 2 — Milestone template (24-week program)

```markdown
### M1 — Baseline Assessment (week 1-6)
- Deliverable: emissions measurement (scope 1, 2, 3); supply chain audit; social + governance baseline
- Validation: credible, third-party-verifiable data in hand
- Target: end of week 6

### M2 — Goal Setting + Framework Selection (week 6-10)
- Deliverable: science-based targets; reporting framework decision (SBTi, TCFD, SASB, etc.); certification plan
- Validation: goals aligned with frameworks; stakeholder sign-off
- Target: end of week 10

### M3 — Operational Improvements (week 8-20)
- Deliverable: supply chain improvements, operational emissions reductions, social policy updates
- Validation: measurable progress against baseline
- Target: end of week 20

### M4 — Certification Process (week 10-22)
- Deliverable: certification applications, audits, improvements to hit thresholds
- Validation: certifications obtained (or specific timeline for obtaining)
- Target: end of week 22

### M5 — Reporting + Communication (week 18-24)
- Deliverable: public sustainability report; website + packaging claims; marketing activation
- Validation: claims legally reviewed; substantiation documented
- Target: end of week 24

### M6 — Ongoing Program + Annual Rhythm (week 24+)
- Deliverable: annual reporting cadence; quarterly internal review; continuous improvement cycle
- Validation: program self-operates; reporting calendar locked
- Target: ongoing
```

## Step 3 — Critical mental model

> **Sustainability claims have asymmetric downside risk.**
>
> A weak claim that's well-substantiated has small upside (customers notice, some prefer). A strong claim that's not substantiated has enormous downside (greenwashing lawsuit, regulatory penalty, customer backlash, brand damage).
>
> The operating rule: claim 20% less than you do; do 20% more than you claim. This builds credibility over time and protects against the rare-but-catastrophic downside.
>
> The brands that get this wrong either: (a) over-claim and get caught (Allbirds sustainability lawsuit, Nestlé greenwashing cases), or (b) do the work but fail to communicate (internal-only ESG programs with no consumer value capture). The middle path — authentic claims about real progress — is the only durable position.

## Step 4 — Domain validators

- **Emissions measurement** — third-party verified or audit-ready
- **Claim substantiation** — every claim has a documented basis
- **Certification maintenance** — audits scheduled
- **Supply-chain visibility** — tier 1 full, tier 2 increasing
- **Legal review** — all public claims reviewed before publish

## Step 8 — Working protocol additions

- **Never claim without substantiation** — file on every claim
- **Never use FTC-scrutinized words generically** — "sustainable" / "eco-friendly" / "green" need specifics
- **Never certify then stop improving** — continuous improvement is part of most certifications
- **Never communicate externally before operations support claims**
- **Always third-party verify** major claims
- **Always coordinate legal + marketing + operations** — siloed ESG programs produce greenwashing
- **Always document progress** — year-over-year narrative is the credibility build
