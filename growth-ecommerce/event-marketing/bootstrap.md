# Event Marketing Program — Bootstrap

> **Inherits from:** `~/.claude/templates/session-kickoff-bootstrap.md`
>
> **Use when:** starting a multi-session event-marketing program — trade shows, conferences, webinars, field events, experiential activations, sponsored events.

---

## Who this is for

Event marketers, field marketers, demand gen leaders at B2B SaaS / B2C brands / consumer-facing businesses running events as part of their go-to-market.

## Opinionated defaults

### Event types

- **Trade shows / conferences (owned booth):** high cost, high intent; measure by pipeline not leads
- **Conference sponsorship (no booth):** awareness + content distribution; measure by brand lift + mentions
- **Owned field events (10-30 target accounts):** highest ROI in B2B; executive dinners, roundtables, VIP experiences
- **Webinars:** scalable content + lead generation; measure by registrants → attendees → pipeline
- **Trade-show side events (hosted breakfasts, parties):** pipeline acceleration at existing prospects
- **Customer conferences:** retention + expansion + advocacy focus
- **Experiential pop-ups (B2C):** brand-building + UGC generation; hard to attribute direct

### Prep timelines

- **Tier-1 trade show (e.g., Dreamforce, CES):** 6 months prep
- **Mid-tier trade show:** 3-4 months
- **Owned field event:** 8-10 weeks
- **Webinar:** 4-6 weeks
- **Customer conference:** 9-12 months

### Pre-event / during-event / post-event split

- **30% effort pre-event** (outreach, registration, content, logistics)
- **20% during** (execution, networking, content capture)
- **50% post-event** (follow-up, content re-use, attribution, retrospective)

Most programs invert this — over-investing in during, under-investing post. This is the #1 event-marketing failure mode.

### Webinar benchmarks

- **Registration → attendance:** 20-40%
- **Attendance → next step:** 10-20%
- **Webinar-to-opportunity:** 3-8% of attendees
- **Best time:** Tuesday-Thursday 11am-2pm attendee-local
- **Duration:** 30-45 min; longer = dropoff
- **Replay:** always make available; 30-60% of pipeline often comes from replay

### Field event benchmarks

- **Target account → registration:** 15-30%
- **Registration → attendance:** 50-70%
- **Attendance → pipeline:** 30-50% (opportunity creation within 90 days)

### Follow-up cadence

- **Day 1:** personalized thank-you with next-step CTA
- **Day 3:** content asset (recording, summary, decklink)
- **Day 7:** proposed next meeting
- **Day 30:** stay-warm touch if no response

### Attribution

- **Multi-touch with event as weighted touch** — events are usually assists, not first-touch
- **Pipeline-generated + pipeline-accelerated** both measured
- **Brand lift measurement** for awareness events where pipeline attribution is weak

### Don't

- **Don't measure trade shows by lead-scan count** — quality over quantity
- **Don't skip post-event retros** — next year's event is built on this year's learnings
- **Don't rely on swag for booth traffic** — swag attracts swag hunters, not buyers
- **Don't send generic post-event email** — personalized outreach has 5-10× response

## Step 0 — Interview question additions

1. **Event portfolio** — which events are being evaluated or committed to?
2. **Budget envelope** — per-event + annual; sponsorship vs. booth vs. owned split
3. **Past event performance** — documented? Pipeline attributed? Retros run?
4. **Event team structure** — in-house, agency-led, hybrid?
5. **Pipeline integration** — how do event leads flow to CRM, SDR, sales?
6. **Owned events capability** — venue list, experience designer, catering ops?
7. **Webinar platform + capacity** — Zoom / ON24 / Livestorm / Demio / Goldcast?
8. **Content capture + re-use** — recording, editing, publishing pipeline?
9. **Executive / SME availability** — can leadership carry the keynote / booth duty?
10. **Partner events** — hosting alongside strategic partners?

## Step 2 — Milestone template (flagship trade show — 24 weeks)

```markdown
### M1 — Strategy + Goal-Setting (week 1-2)
- Deliverable: event goals (pipeline, brand, retention); target account list; booth + sponsorship budget
- Validation: stakeholder sign-off; KPIs measurable
- Target: end of week 2

### M2 — Creative + Logistics (week 2-10)
- Deliverable: booth design, swag, demo setup, speaker prep, collateral
- Validation: all assets in production; booth mock-up approved
- Target: end of week 10

### M3 — Pre-Event Outreach (week 10-20)
- Deliverable: meeting scheduling with target accounts; pre-show content (blog, social, email)
- Validation: meetings confirmed; awareness campaign live
- Target: end of week 20

### M4 — Event Execution (week 24)
- Deliverable: booth staffed, meetings held, content captured, lead-qualification live
- Validation: meetings happened; leads captured with qualification notes
- Target: event week

### M5 — Post-Event Follow-up (week 24-28)
- Deliverable: personalized follow-ups day 1 / 3 / 7; content re-use (recap blog, social, email)
- Validation: follow-up cadence complete; content distributed
- Target: end of week 28

### M6 — Attribution + Retrospective (week 28-29)
- Deliverable: pipeline attribution; brand-lift measurement (if applicable); full retrospective
- Validation: next-year decision (attend / skip / expand) made with data
- Target: end of week 29
```

## Step 3 — Critical mental model

> **Events are pipeline accelerators, not pipeline generators — in most cases.**
>
> With rare exceptions (hosted owned events with curated target-account lists), events are not efficient acquisition channels. A $200K booth at a tier-1 trade show rarely pays back on new-pipeline alone.
>
> What events excel at: (1) advancing existing opportunities through face-to-face time, (2) brand-building at scale, (3) retention and expansion at customer conferences, (4) content-generation opportunities.
>
> Measuring events on new-pipeline alone leads to cutting them when they still deliver value elsewhere. Measuring on multi-dimensional value — pipeline acceleration + brand + retention + content — gives an honest read.

## Step 4 — Domain validators

- **Goal clarity:** pipeline goal, brand goal, retention goal — each measurable
- **Pre-event meeting scheduling:** target % of account-list meetings booked before event
- **Follow-up cadence completion:** 100% of captured leads receive cadence
- **Attribution measurable:** events contribute visible weight in multi-touch model
- **Retrospective documented:** every event has a post-event retro within 2 weeks

## Step 8 — Working protocol additions

- **Never commit to trade show without pipeline + brand goals defined**
- **Never skip post-event retro** — next year is built on this year
- **Never rely on swag to drive booth traffic**
- **Never send generic follow-up** — personalize or don't bother
- **Always schedule meetings pre-event** — don't rely on random booth traffic
- **Always capture content** — every event is a content asset; plan for it
- **Always measure multi-dimensionally** — pipeline + brand + retention
