## Use cases

- **Retailer landscape research** — which retailers fit the brand, in what sequence?
- **Buyer research** — specific buyers at target retailer; responsibilities, background, recent decisions
- **Category review timing research** — when are category reviews at target retailers?
- **Trade-spend benchmarks** — co-op, slotting, demo, promo norms in your category
- **Chargeback benchmarks** — what's typical, what's high
- **Broker / rep evaluation** — category-specific representatives; coverage + track record
- **Retail-partner competitive analysis** — who else is on shelf; positioning; velocity

## Opinionated source priorities

**Prioritize:**

- **Retailer investor relations + earnings calls** — channel strategy, category focus, private-label ambitions
- **Trade publications** — Progressive Grocer (grocery), Winsight (C-store), SGB Media (outdoor), Beauty Matter (beauty), Pet Age (pet)
- **RangeMe + ECRM** — retailer-buyer platforms where buyers share criteria + open categories
- **Circana (formerly IRI + NPD)** — category scanner data (paid)
- **Nielsen IQ** — CPG channel data (paid)
- **Spins** — natural / specialty channel data (paid)
- **Retail-specific LinkedIn communities** — buyer networks, category managers, retail-sales operators

**De-prioritize:**

- Generic "how to pitch to Target" listicles
- Broker-published success stories (marketing bias)
- AI-generated retail-strategy content

## Question-set specializations

### For retailer landscape:

1. Target retailer universe for category — specialty, mass, club, etc.
2. Door count + revenue per store for each
3. Category size and sub-category focus at retailer
4. Private-label presence + competitive threat
5. Brand-partner slots typically available
6. Buyer patience / relationship expectations
7. Geographic coverage + demographics
8. Margin / trade-spend norms

### For buyer research:

1. Named buyer(s) by category at target retailer
2. LinkedIn + press background — tenure, prior roles, recent decisions
3. Speaking / interview history — what do they say publicly about category?
4. Recent brand decisions at their retailer (adds / drops / moves)
5. Known personal preferences / red flags

### For category review timing:

1. Category review calendar at target retailers
2. Typical pitch-to-decision timeline
3. Windows open / closed in next 12-18 months
4. Prep time required (pitch decks, samples, category data)

### For trade-spend benchmarks:

1. Co-op typical % of wholesale revenue in your category / retailer
2. Slotting fees (if applicable) by retailer + category
3. Demo program cost + typical ROI
4. In-store promo depth norms
5. Planogram real estate cost (if applicable)
