## Blocker questions

1. **Is internal alignment locked on positioning?** If leadership disagrees, design work is premature — re-align first.
2. **Any trademark / legal issue on new name / mark?** Resolve before visual build.
3. **Any retail partner timeline hard-stop?** Retail resets happen twice a year; miss the window and wait 6 months.
4. **Any customer research contradicting the direction?** Don't bury contradiction; decide whether to update direction or explain why research is discounted.
5. **Any active PR / brand event** that should coordinate with launch timing?

## Ranked work patterns

### If positioning is pre-alignment
Do not move to visual / voice work. Alignment first. Options:
1. Workshop with leadership to surface disagreements
2. Customer research to ground the argument in data
3. Stakeholder interviews to understand what they're really defending
4. Positioning options test (3 versions, show customers) to let data decide

### If positioning is locked, visual is pending
Standard sequence: moodboards → primary direction → logo → color → type → photography → application → asset library.

### If visual is done, rollout is pending
Coordinate:
1. Internal team training
2. Retail / wholesale partner lead time (packaging, POP)
3. DTC site update + email templates
4. Amazon / marketplace listings
5. Social channels
6. PR + launch moment

### If post-launch measurement is needed
Brand tracker: unaided awareness, aided awareness, consideration, preference, NPS — monthly baseline for 12+ months post-launch to measure equity trend.

## Working protocol additions

- **Never ship visual work while positioning is in debate**
- **Never skip retail partner lead times**
- **Never launch rebrand without post-launch brand tracker** — you need measurement
- **Never skip legal review** on new names / marks / claims
- **Always document every choice** — 3 years later, someone will ask why

## Common drift patterns

- **Stakeholder-driven visual creep** — every exec adds a requirement; ends up bland
- **Late-stage positioning reopening** — reset the project; resist
- **Research over-indexing** — customer research is input, not decision; brand leadership must still decide
- **Retail sync underestimation** — retail partners don't flex; plan accordingly
- **Post-launch measurement deferral** — without tracker, you can't know if it worked
