## Who this is for

Brand leaders, marketing VPs, founders running brand-positioning work. Programs span 12-24 weeks — rebrands are not quick projects. Positioning refreshes (lighter work) 8-12 weeks.

## Opinionated defaults

### Positioning philosophy

- **Positioning statement structure:** "For [target], [brand] is the [category] that [distinct value], because [reason to believe]."
- **Specificity beats inclusivity.** "For busy parents of kids 4-10 in suburban US who value schedule stability" beats "for families."
- **Distinctiveness over differentiation.** Customers rarely compare feature-by-feature; they recognize a brand or they don't.
- **Category entry points over personas.** What memory / occasion / situation triggers the customer to need your category? Those are what you must own.

### Brand architecture

- **Single master brand:** default for most; simplest to build equity
- **Endorsed brands:** sub-brands endorsed by master (Marriott → Courtyard)
- **House of brands:** independent portfolio (P&G, Unilever); requires scale to support
- **Don't split brand architecture without scale to fund** — each brand needs its own marketing

### Visual identity discipline

- **Logo: works at 16px, monochrome, on any background.** If it fails these, redesign.
- **Color: 1 primary + 1-2 secondary + neutrals.** More is indulgent.
- **Type: 1-2 typefaces.** Hero + body. More creates visual noise.
- **Photography: one mood + one technical spec (lighting, composition, grading).** Consistency over variety.
- **Motion: brand-level principles, not ad-hoc.** How does the brand move?

### Voice discipline

- **Principles + examples + anti-examples.** "We are X. We say this. We never say that."
- **Voice chart: 3-5 attributes with behavioral definitions.** Not "friendly" — "uses contractions, addresses the reader directly, ends statements with periods not exclamations."

### Messaging hierarchy

- **Tagline** (3-7 words) — distillation
- **One-liner** (1 sentence) — elevator pitch
- **Paragraph** (50-100 words) — about page
- **Long-form** (300-500 words) — company narrative

Each derived from the others; not independent.

### Research required

- **15-30 customer interviews** — qualitative, mix of current / churned / never-bought
- **Competitive audit of top 10-15 brands** — positioning map on 2 axes
- **Positioning workshop with stakeholders** — mis-alignment inside the company is the #1 source of drift
- **Prototype testing** — show positioning options to real buyers before committing

### Rollout sequence

- **Internal first** — team must live it before customers see it
- **Existing customers second** — they have brand expectations; transition carefully
- **New audiences third** — fresh impressions; highest leverage
- **Retail / wholesale partners** — need sync for visual changes (POP, packaging, POS)
- **Amazon / DTC site** — listing, A+ Content, PDP updates

### Timing + co-ordination

- **Don't rebrand mid-crisis** — looks reactive; customers lose trust
- **Don't rebrand every 2 years** — erases equity
- **5-7 year horizon** for major rebrands
- **Pricing / promotion changes** — don't do in same quarter as rebrand; too many signal changes at once

## Step 0 — Interview question additions

1. **What's driving the rebrand?** New positioning, new audience, M&A integration, equity erosion, crisis response, platform expansion?
2. **Current brand state** — how well-defined is positioning, voice, visual identity, architecture?
3. **Research on hand** — customer interviews, brand-health studies, positioning audits?
4. **Internal alignment** — does leadership agree on direction? Where's the disagreement?
5. **Scope** — full rebrand (logo, name, visual, voice, positioning) or positioning-only refresh?
6. **Budget + team** — in-house creative, agency-led, or hybrid?
7. **Timeline constraint** — specific launch event (retail reset, new product launch) forcing timing?
8. **Equity at stake** — established brand with customer recognition vs. nascent brand?
9. **Retail / wholesale exposure** — POP materials, packaging to change? Lead time?
10. **Trademark / legal considerations** — name changes? Clearance status?

## Step 2 — Milestone template (20-week rebrand)

```markdown
### M1 — Discovery + Research (week 1-4)
- Deliverable: customer interviews (15-30); competitive audit; brand-health baseline; positioning hypotheses
- Validation: interview synthesis; positioning options mapped
- Target: end of week 4

### M2 — Positioning Lock (week 4-6)
- Deliverable: positioning statement; messaging hierarchy; voice chart; brand architecture decision
- Validation: internal stakeholders aligned; positioning test with customers
- Target: end of week 6

### M3 — Visual Identity (week 6-10)
- Deliverable: logo, color, type, photography direction, motion principles, design system
- Validation: design tested at all scales + contexts (digital, retail, packaging)
- Target: end of week 10

### M4 — Application + Asset Library (week 10-14)
- Deliverable: packaging, site, email templates, ad templates, retail POP, sales materials
- Validation: asset library complete; templates usable by team
- Target: end of week 14

### M5 — Internal Rollout (week 14-16)
- Deliverable: team training; internal brand guidelines; FAQ; change-management plan
- Validation: team demonstrates brand fluency; stakeholders aligned on messaging
- Target: end of week 16

### M6 — External Launch (week 16-20)
- Deliverable: customer / market launch; retail / wholesale partner sync; PR coordination
- Validation: launch executed; brand tracker live; post-launch sentiment monitored
- Target: end of week 20
```

## Step 3 — Critical mental model

> **Brand is a memory structure, not a design exercise.**
>
> Customers don't memorize your positioning statement. They memorize: one color, one name, one face, one situation where you fit. Your job is to make those memory units distinctive, consistent, and repeatedly encountered.
>
> Rebrands fail when they prioritize design novelty over memory structure continuity. A visually "better" logo that erases the mental brand asset destroys equity — even if it wins design awards.
>
> The question to ask for every rebrand choice: "Does this make the brand easier to recognize, remember, and retrieve — or does it reset the clock?"

## Step 4 — Domain validators

- **Positioning test:** show positioning statement to 10 current customers + 10 non-customers; can they repeat the core idea 24 hours later?
- **Visual test:** logo + primary color + one image at 16px, 500px, billboard scale — distinctive?
- **Voice test:** 3 writers produce 5 sample paragraphs each from voice chart; do they sound like the same brand?
- **Retail test:** shelf mockup at arms-length; does the brand stand out?
- **Legal test:** trademark clearance; domain availability; social handle availability

## Step 8 — Working protocol additions

- **Never rebrand in response to a single crisis** — it looks reactive
- **Never launch a rebrand without internal alignment** — mixed messages confuse customers
- **Never ignore customer research** — your taste is not data
- **Never rush retail partners** — they need 90+ days lead time for packaging / POP updates
- **Always test at scale** — what looks good at 500px may fail at 16px or billboard
- **Always document the rationale** — 3 years later, someone will ask "why did we pick X?"
