# Brand Positioning — Deep Research Template

> **Inherits from:** `~/.claude/templates/deep-research-competitive-landscape.md`

---

## Use cases

- **Competitive brand landscape** — positioning map of category players; axes + occupied space + white-space
- **Customer interview synthesis** — post-interview pattern extraction
- **Category entry-point research** — what memory / situation / occasion triggers category need?
- **Brand-health benchmarking** — awareness, consideration, NPS at competitors
- **Name / mark clearance research** — trademark availability + linguistic / cultural screening
- **Historical rebrand case studies** — successful rebrands + failures + lessons

## Opinionated source priorities

**Prioritize:**

- **Ehrenberg-Bass research** — How Brands Grow (Byron Sharp); category-entry-points methodology
- **Les Binet + Peter Field** — "The Long and the Short of It" for brand-vs-performance frameworks
- **YouGov BrandIndex / Morning Consult Brand Intelligence** — brand-tracker benchmarks
- **Interbrand Best Global Brands** — annual brand valuation methodology + case studies
- **Industry-specific brand research** — Beauty Matter (beauty), BoF (fashion), Adweek (consumer)
- **Public rebrand case studies** — JCPenney's failed rebrand, Tropicana's failed rebrand, Mastercard's successful refresh, Airbnb's "Bélo" — documented in industry press

**De-prioritize:**

- Design-agency "philosophy" content (marketing)
- Generic brand-strategy consulting frameworks
- "Top 10 rebrand fails" listicles (often miss the why)

## Question-set specializations

### For competitive brand landscape:

1. Top-15 brands in category
2. Positioning statements (extracted from copy, not from "About Us" claims)
3. Visual identity axes — color spectrum, type style, photo mood
4. Voice spectrum — formal/casual, serious/playful, authoritative/collaborative
5. Category entry points each brand tries to own
6. Price-positioning map
7. Brand-architecture patterns (master vs. portfolio)
8. Recent rebrands in category — who, when, why, how received

### For category entry-point research:

1. What occasions, situations, or memories trigger category demand?
2. What functional + emotional jobs does the category serve?
3. Which brands already own which entry points?
4. Under-owned entry points — where's the white-space?
5. Cultural / generational shifts affecting entry-point relevance

### For brand-health benchmarking:

1. Unaided awareness — top-of-mind recall
2. Aided awareness — prompted recognition
3. Consideration — "would buy next time"
4. NPS / advocacy
5. Category-leadership perception — "the best at X"
6. Trust / credibility markers
7. Trend direction — gaining / flat / losing equity

### For rebrand case studies:

1. Pre-rebrand state — what problem was it solving?
2. Execution — scope, timing, rollout
3. Short-term signal (first 90 days) — customer reaction, sales, PR
4. Long-term outcome (2-5 years) — did equity rebuild or erode?
5. Key decisions that worked / didn't
6. Warnings for future rebranders
