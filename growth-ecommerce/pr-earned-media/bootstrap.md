# PR / Earned Media Program — Bootstrap

> **Inherits from:** `~/.claude/templates/session-kickoff-bootstrap.md`
>
> **Use when:** launching or overhauling a PR program — media relations, tier-1 press outreach, trade press engagement, executive thought leadership, crisis communications readiness.

---

## Who this is for

Communications leaders, CMOs, founders at DTC / B2B / consumer-facing brands. Programs span 12-24 weeks to establish rhythm, ongoing after.

## Opinionated defaults

### PR philosophy

- **PR is demand-generation-adjacent, not demand-generation.** Measure it on brand lift + pipeline influence, not on direct MQLs.
- **Earned media takes time to compound.** 6-12 months before rhythm delivers consistent coverage.
- **Tier-1 press requires story, not pitch.** Journalists want stories (original data, founder insight, category-defining moment), not announcements.
- **Trade press requires relationships.** Ongoing value exchange, not just press releases.

### Measurement

- **Primary metrics:** mentions × reach × sentiment × source quality
- **Secondary metrics:** SEO impact (backlinks, domain authority), pipeline influence, share-of-voice vs. competitors
- **Anti-metrics:** press-release count alone, AVE (advertising value equivalent — widely discredited)
- **Pipeline attribution:** weighted indirect credit in multi-touch; PR is an assist, rarely a source

### Tier-1 press vs. trade press vs. long-form

- **Tier-1 (WSJ, NYT, Bloomberg, Forbes, Fortune, FT):** longer lead times (3-6 weeks+), higher brand lift, harder to land; pitch via longer-cycle relationship
- **Trade press (Adweek, Modern Retail, TechCrunch, The Information, category-specific):** faster turnaround, deeper industry penetration
- **Long-form / podcasts / video:** founders + executives; thought-leadership focus
- **Owned media** (company blog, LinkedIn, Substack): substitute when earned is slow; amplifies earned when it happens

### Thought leadership

- **Executive bylines:** ghost-written OK, but executive must stand behind every claim
- **Original data > opinion:** "We surveyed X customers and found Y" lands better than "I think Z"
- **Consistency:** monthly cadence builds reputation; sporadic bursts do not
- **Conference speaking:** 2-4 tier-1 talks/year for credibility; more for scale

### Crisis PR readiness

- **Rehearsed response plans** — not "we'll figure it out when it happens"
- **Speed matters:** first hour of response shapes narrative
- **Spokesperson assigned + trained** — typically CEO for major crises; comms lead for operational
- **Legal + communications coordination** — drafted statements, pre-approved paths
- **Internal communication first** — team must know before customers/press

### Founder-driven PR

- **3× more effective than hired spokesperson** in early-stage brands
- **Founder becomes the brand's voice;** scaling past this is hard — succession plan needed
- **Founder time: 10-15 hours/month** sustainable; more is burnout

### Don'ts

- **Don't send generic press releases** — dead-letter territory
- **Don't pay for pay-to-play coverage** — destroys credibility
- **Don't count AVE as a metric** — PR industry has moved past it
- **Don't announce before the story exists** — hype without substance becomes a negative story later
- **Don't ignore trade press for tier-1** — trade press built the foundation tier-1 cites

## Step 0 — Interview question additions

1. **Current PR state** — agency, in-house, hybrid, none?
2. **Recent coverage** — what, where, how obtained?
3. **Story assets** — original data, founder insight, category firsts, customer stories?
4. **Executive availability** — who can carry interviews / bylines / conferences?
5. **Crisis history** — any past incidents + handling lessons?
6. **Relationships** — any existing journalist / analyst relationships to build on?
7. **Budget envelope** — PR agency retainer, content production, event sponsorship?
8. **Measurement baseline** — share of voice, sentiment, reach?
9. **Category moment / news peg** — industry event, regulatory, M&A, product launch?
10. **Competitive PR intensity** — how much noise does the category have?

## Step 2 — Milestone template (16-week program)

```markdown
### M1 — Strategy + Story Development (week 1-3)
- Deliverable: narrative platform, 3-5 hero stories; executive voice development; target outlet list
- Validation: stories pressure-tested for news value; outlets prioritized
- Target: end of week 3

### M2 — Relationship Building (week 3-8)
- Deliverable: journalist outreach, relationship calendar; trade press pitches; thought leadership planning
- Validation: 20+ journalist conversations initiated; first trade press coverage
- Target: end of week 8

### M3 — Original Research + Data (week 4-10)
- Deliverable: original data project (survey / customer data / industry analysis); companion pitch narrative
- Validation: data published; first tier-2 or tier-1 pickup
- Target: end of week 10

### M4 — Thought Leadership Cadence (week 8-14)
- Deliverable: monthly executive byline; 2-4 podcasts / speaking slots / bylines
- Validation: cadence sustained; sentiment tracking
- Target: end of week 14

### M5 — Crisis Plan + Playbook (week 12-15)
- Deliverable: crisis communications plan, spokesperson training, pre-approved statements
- Validation: dry-run scenarios completed; team trained
- Target: end of week 15

### M6 — Measurement + Ongoing Rhythm (week 15-16+)
- Deliverable: monthly PR report, quarterly reviews, brand-tracker integration
- Validation: program self-operates; measurement dashboard live
- Target: end of week 16+
```

## Step 3 — Critical mental model

> **Earned media is a credibility bank that compounds — if you make regular deposits.**
>
> Every story, byline, podcast, or mention is a deposit. The balance grows slowly at first and exponentially after 12-18 months of consistency. When a tier-1 outlet finally writes about your category, the brands with years of deposits get cited; the brands with sporadic deposits get ignored.
>
> Short-term PR (PR push around a launch) gets a one-week bump and returns to baseline. Long-term PR (sustained story-telling + relationship-building) shifts share-of-voice, brand perception, and earned-opportunity rate permanently.
>
> Brands that measure PR on launch bumps always under-invest. Brands that measure on compound credibility gain market position.

## Step 4 — Domain validators

- **Story pipeline:** 3-5 active stories at any time; no gaps
- **Journalist relationship roster:** 30+ active; quarterly review
- **Thought-leadership cadence:** 1+ executive piece/month minimum
- **Share-of-voice tracking:** monthly vs. competitors
- **Sentiment tracking:** monthly direction
- **Crisis plan tested:** quarterly dry-run

## Step 8 — Working protocol additions

- **Never send generic press release** — lead with story
- **Never pay for coverage** — destroys credibility
- **Never announce before the story exists** — hype without substance is worse than silence
- **Never count AVE** — discredited metric
- **Always lead with original data / insight** in tier-1 pitches
- **Always build trade press relationships** — foundation for tier-1
- **Always coordinate crisis response** with legal + leadership
