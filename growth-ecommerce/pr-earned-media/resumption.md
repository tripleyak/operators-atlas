## Blocker questions

1. **Any active crisis or brand-risk event?** Pause planned campaigns; focus on response
2. **Any embargoed story / announcement in flight?** Coordinate timing carefully
3. **Any executive availability issue affecting planned outreach?**
4. **Any competitive PR moment** (competitor funding / launch / lawsuit) that changes share-of-voice picture?
5. **Any legal review pending** on a planned statement / quote?

## Ranked work patterns

### If crisis or brand-risk active
Speed + discipline:
1. Assess — scope, audience, speed of spread
2. Contain — control narrative where possible
3. Respond — on-the-record statement approved by legal + leadership
4. Follow up — monitor, adjust, retrospect
5. Document — post-crisis learnings

Pause planned outreach during active response.

### If pitch response is low
Diagnostic:
1. Is the story actually newsworthy? (often not)
2. Are you pitching the right journalists? (check recent bylines, beats)
3. Is timing right? (news cycles, industry events, competitor noise)
4. Is pitch quality strong? (brevity, story-first, follow-up)
5. Is the target's beat changing?

### If thought-leadership cadence drifting
Causes:
1. Executive bandwidth
2. Topic exhaustion
3. Editorial support gap
4. Unclear measurement → low priority

Reset cadence; restructure ghost-writing support.

### Monthly PR review
Standard:
1. Coverage log — quantity + tier + sentiment
2. Share-of-voice vs. competitors
3. Story pipeline — active + planned
4. Journalist relationships — engagement, growth
5. Byline pipeline
6. Upcoming news pegs

## Working protocol additions

- **Never respond to crisis without legal coordination**
- **Never pitch without actual story** — journalists blacklist
- **Never break embargo** — destroys trust
- **Never rely on AVE** — metric is discredited
- **Always lead with data** where possible
- **Always follow up journalist relationships** — quarterly check-in even without pitch
- **Always document embargoed stories** — avoid accidental leaks

## Common drift patterns

- **Press-release-only PR** — dead letters; journalists ignore
- **Metric over-simplification** — "mentions" count without sentiment / reach
- **Founder-dependency** — no succession plan; program collapses when founder exits
- **Crisis complacency** — no dry-runs; real crisis is disaster
- **Pay-to-play temptations** — always destroys credibility long-term
