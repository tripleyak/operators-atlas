## Use cases

- **Journalist / outlet research** — top-20 journalists + outlets covering your category
- **PR agency evaluation** — agency shortlist + pitch + contract
- **Competitor coverage teardown** — share of voice, sentiment, story types
- **Tier-1 pitch research** — what works for WSJ / NYT / Bloomberg in your category
- **Thought leadership benchmarks** — byline cadence, platform mix at peer brands
- **Crisis case studies** — how brands handled specific crisis types

## Opinionated source priorities

**Prioritize:**

- **Muck Rack** — journalist database, editorial calendars, pitch data
- **Cision** — journalist + outlet database
- **ProfNet / HARO** — journalist source requests
- **The Information** — trade-press coverage patterns
- **Media outlets' editorial masthead + beats pages** — direct source
- **Press Gazette / Nieman Lab** — journalism-industry research
- **PR Council / PR Week** — agency + industry benchmarks

**De-prioritize:**

- PR agency sales decks
- Generic "how to get PR" content
- AI-generated pitch templates

## Question-set specializations

### For journalist / outlet research:

1. Top-20 journalists covering your category; beats + recent pieces + pitch preferences
2. Top-10 outlets + typical story format + editorial calendar
3. Tier-1 vs. trade press mix for your category
4. Journalist turnover — who's leaving, who's joining
5. Contact patterns — email preferred, social DMs, phone, pitch forms

### For agency evaluation:

1. Agency category specialization
2. Client portfolio + published results
3. Pricing model — retainer + project + performance
4. Team structure — senior vs. associates
5. Media relationships — depth in tier-1 vs. trade
6. Speaking + event support
7. Crisis management track record

### For competitor coverage teardown:

1. Volume of coverage — last 12 months
2. Tier mix — tier-1 vs. trade vs. long-tail
3. Story types — launch, funding, thought leadership, category-defining
4. Executive visibility — who's quoted, who's podcasting
5. Thought leadership — bylines, speaking, original research
6. Crisis handling (if any)

### For tier-1 pitch research:

1. What's worked at tier-1 in your category — specific story angles
2. Editorial cycles + calendar
3. Relationship expectations — direct vs. agency
4. Embargoes vs. exclusives
5. Follow-up norms

### For thought leadership benchmarks:

1. Byline cadence at peer brands
2. Byline outlets — owned vs. trade vs. tier-1
3. Podcast appearances
4. Conference speaking
5. Original research / reports produced
